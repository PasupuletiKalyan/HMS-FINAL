MAHINDRA UNIVERSITY

ANTI-DRUG, SUBSTANCE, AND ALCOHOL USE POLICY

Hostel Management Department

1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes Mahindra University's commitment to maintaining a safe, healthy, and productive environment for all students, faculty, and staff. The University strictly prohibits the use, possession, distribution, or sale of illegal drugs, controlled substances, and alcohol within campus premises, including all hostel facilities.

This policy applies to all students residing in or visiting university hostels, regardless of age, and is in effect at all times throughout the academic year, including holidays and vacations when the hostels remain open.

2. Prohibited Substances and Activities

The following are prohibited in all hostel facilities and university premises:

  • Illegal Drugs: Possession, use, manufacture, distribution, or sale of any narcotic, controlled substance, or drug paraphernalia is strictly prohibited.
  • Prescription Medications: Use of prescription medications without valid medical prescription or use in a manner inconsistent with the prescribed directions.
  • Alcohol: Possession, consumption, distribution, or sale of alcoholic beverages is prohibited in all hostel facilities, regardless of a student's age.
  • Smoking and Tobacco: Use of cigarettes, e-cigarettes, vaping devices, and all tobacco products is prohibited within hostel premises.
  • Intoxication: Being under the influence of alcohol or illegal substances while on university premises or at university-sponsored events.

3. Enforcement and Disciplinary Actions

Violations of this policy will result in immediate disciplinary action, which may include:

  1. Verbal and written warnings
  2. Mandatory counseling and substance abuse programs
  3. Disciplinary probation
  4. Suspension from university housing
  5. Suspension from the university
  6. Expulsion from the university
  7. Referral to law enforcement authorities for criminal prosecution

The severity of sanctions will depend on the nature, frequency, and severity of the violation, as well as the student's disciplinary history.

4. Room Inspections

The University reserves the right to conduct room inspections with reasonable suspicion of policy violations. Such inspections may be conducted by authorized university personnel, including hostel wardens and security officers, in accordance with university procedures and applicable laws.

5. Reporting Procedures

Students are encouraged to report any suspected violations of this policy to hostel wardens, resident advisors, or security personnel. The University is committed to maintaining confidentiality to the extent possible when handling such reports.

6. Support Services

The University offers counseling and support services for students dealing with substance abuse issues. Students are encouraged to seek help voluntarily before violations occur. Information about these services can be obtained from the Student Counseling Center.

7. Policy Acknowledgment

All students are required to acknowledge receipt and understanding of this policy through the signed affidavit submitted during the hostel registration process. This acknowledgment confirms that the student agrees to abide by all terms of this policy.
